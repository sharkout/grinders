About us

Grinders, you say?

G4 Grinders F.C. is a local football club, sited at a small-sized park in g4 block, WAPDA, Lahore. It all started back in the year 2006 when some kids from around the block started playing football here. The neighborhood never anticipated that time nears when the park would become a home-ground of a football club. Grinders are ragtag and so is their game. Not exactly. But, there are nubs in a game which can only be discerned if you are a G block resident.
Although, there has not been any sort of legal permission passed by the local commission probably due to lack of dissemination, the group (G4 Grinders) has been obliged to carry out a transitional set-up that includes the autonomy to play football within G4 park.

Inside Out

We believe in persistence and hard-work. Talent is something that is deemed as a precocious condiment within our culture. The elder role is played by how much a man (or woman. Yes, we strongly support gender equality) works with the ball. We play thrice a week. Our favorite game-style is futsal. We have won countless number of matches and by that same token, lost too. We love "Coke"! It is true. We love coke so much that if we are, under impossible or damned circumstances, behind by 10 goals from our opponents, and you happen to wager a bottle of Coke on our win at that very instant, within the next 15 minutes the word "behind" will change into "lead". The abridged version of it all is "We play good football".

Our History

Couple of years back, before 2006, a recreational park, in G4 block, was built under the supervision of Wapda Town Resource Allocation Unit. Rules for the usage of this small-sized park were inscribed on a board just beside the park-entrance, clearly mentioning, "Playing football, cricket and any other sport on this field is prohibited."
A few months after its creation, some juveniles started playing football in the facility. Now, that was a problem for the neighborhood because, every time the boys were ignorant, they'd mistakenly hit the ball, tossing it into homes, parallel to the park. People in the surroundings got vexed with the intractable situation of "football". They began calling security officers to deal with the fractious soccer-kids.
Time passed and the lads continued their act. Officers showed up many times, trying to stop them from playing in the park, but they were stubborn as hell. Instead of alleviation, they aggravated the dispute by tugging the "rules-board" off the ground. After this obnoxious event, the security badges became even more malevolently inclined

towards the contrary. They would pay surprise visits at the park to spook them boys. A day came when the security-guards gave up scouting around for football players.
Eventually, the number of footballers escalated and game-chemistry developed. It was becoming a football club. In 2010, Saad, one of the best G4 had to offer, left playing in G4. In the same year, they enhanced their style and skill, and began setting matches with teams from different blocks of the same town. Every team they played against would win; no matter how hard they tried or what new skills they brought in the field. The results proved rather discouraging. It was not very late when another depressive "Shahrukh", attempted to leave the crew. But, there were folks who cajoled him into staying with the team.
A year later, in 2013, the same player who tried to abandon the club, played formidable football. He would persistently toil with the ball and not lose it until he was totally worn out. Whenever, he had the ball under his right-foot, they used to be like, "Hell...Motor's got the ball now", "When is it going

to stop?!" He and his teammates fought really hard with themselves and took the team to whole another level by defeating every soccer team of the town in less than a month of that year.
Thereafter, when the idea of making club more organized popped up in their heads, it was knotty to choose the right name for the team. Now, the word "grind" is an informal word for "work" (or toil) and a "Grinder" works on the principle of an electric motor. The three words, "grind", "work" and "motor", appeared to be much associated with each other and that is how it stimulated the guy called "Motor" to advice a name for the club "G4-Grinders F.C." which was unconditionally accepted as the official title of the G-Block footballers' crew.
Today, we aren't the best soccer team in town. But, we are relentless. We are ferocious. We are audacious.
WE ARE GRINDERS!
